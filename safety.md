# Safety at InnerRing

**Last Updated: July 19, 2026**

InnerRing is a privacy-first, invite-only, 1:1 messaging app for close friends and family. This page explains how to report concerns, block a contact, and get help.

## How Safety Works

InnerRing uses end-to-end encryption for ordinary messages. Messages are encrypted before they leave your device, and InnerRing cannot routinely read their plaintext or proactively scan ordinary chats.

InnerRing instead uses user reporting, blocking, rate limits, account restrictions where justified, and proportionate review procedures. The content and conduct that are not allowed are described in our [Terms of Service](terms.html).

## Reporting

You can report:

- A received message from the message bubble menu.
- A contact's display name from that contact's profile.

Reporting a message sends InnerRing a plaintext copy of that selected message and report metadata for safety review. Although the report is protected in transit by HTTPS/TLS, the reported copy is no longer protected from InnerRing by message end-to-end encryption. The selected message is hidden from your normal chat view after the report is submitted successfully.

Reporting a display name sends InnerRing the selected profile name and report metadata.

For child sexual exploitation, grooming, or urgent child-safeguarding concerns, the app may ask whether anyone involved is in the United Kingdom, the United States, or whether that is unknown. InnerRing does not collect global location data to verify this information.

InnerRing reviews only the information available for a report. A report is not treated as proof by itself. Reports involving immediate danger, child safeguarding, child sexual exploitation, grooming, or credible threats are prioritized.

Information submitted in a report is handled and retained as described in our [Privacy Policy](privacy.html).

## Blocking

Blocking a contact stops normal interaction with that contact where supported.

Blocking a reported message is separate from blocking a contact. After you successfully report a message, InnerRing blocks that selected message from your normal chat view. Reporting a message does not block the contact.

Blocking does not necessarily delete records needed for delivery, synchronization, security, safety review, legal compliance, or record keeping.

## Staying Safe

Be cautious when opening links sent in messages. Links may be used for scams, impersonation, payment fraud, malware, or recovery-key theft. If a message seems unusual, verify the sender through another trusted method before acting.

Never share your recovery key. InnerRing will not ask you to send it in a message, by email, or through an external link.

## Urgent Help

InnerRing is not an emergency, police, fraud, or child-protection service. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. You may also submit an in-app report so InnerRing can review and preserve the available report information.

For users in the United Kingdom:

- Call 999 if there is immediate danger.
- For non-emergency police matters, use 101 or the relevant local police route.
- For fraud or cybercrime, consider [Action Fraud](https://www.actionfraud.police.uk/) and contact your bank or payment provider if money was involved.
- For child-protection concerns, contact emergency services, police, or an appropriate child-protection service.

For users in the United States:

- Call 911 if there is immediate danger.
- For suspected child exploitation, consider the [NCMEC CyberTipline](https://report.cybertip.org/).
- For cyber-enabled fraud or scams, consider [FBI IC3](https://www.ic3.gov/) or [FTC ReportFraud](https://reportfraud.ftc.gov/), and contact your bank or payment provider if money was involved.
- For self-harm or emotional-crisis support, call or text [988](https://988lifeline.org/).

## Complaints and Contact

Use the in-app reporting tools to report a message or display name. They are the safest way to provide the relevant content and account context.

You may complain about how InnerRing handled or failed to handle a safety report, a safety decision affecting you, difficulty using the reporting process, or how our safety rules were applied. The definitive complaints procedure is set out in our [Terms of Service](terms.html).

Email [innerring.app@gmail.com](mailto:innerring.app@gmail.com) with the subject "Safety complaint." Include the report ID if you have it, what you want reviewed, and why you believe the outcome should change. Do not send your recovery key.

Email alone is not sufficient to verify account ownership. Identity-sensitive action may require verification through the signed-in app. Privacy complaints and regulator contact options are described in our [Privacy Policy](privacy.html).
